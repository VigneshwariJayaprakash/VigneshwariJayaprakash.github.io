My Journey

I began my professional career in 2013 at Infosys, where I spent over five years progressing into a Senior Software Engineer role, working on multi-tier backend systems and data pipelines for large enterprise clients. My work focused on designing scalable, reliable architectures that supported complex business operations across sourcing, finance, and logistics domains.

After relocating to the United States for personal reasons, I took a deliberate career pause and used that time to upskill in advanced areas including machine learning, deep learning, and generative AI. This period of focused learning allowed me to deepen my technical foundation and pivot more fully into data science, preparing me for advanced, analytics-driven roles.

I am currently a graduate student in the Master of Science in Data Science (Computing and Decision Analytics) program at Arizona State University, where I maintain a 4.0 GPA and focus on applied machine learning, statistical modeling, and data-driven decision systems.

Previously, I worked with the New Mexico Department of Information Technology as a Data Scientist, where I applied my software engineering background to build production-grade analytics and AI solutions, including machine learning models and GenAI-enabled systems used for operational decision-making.

I'm particularly interested in roles that sit at the intersection of software engineering and data science, where robust systems and rigorous analytics come together to solve real-world problems.
